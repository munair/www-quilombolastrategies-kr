FAQ

What is Capoeira?

Capoeira is an Afro-Brazilian art form that incorporates rhythmic music, dance, core-strengthening kicks, and flexibility-improving escapes into playful expression. Capoeira is a game. Friends play-fight in front of a crowd gathered in a ring, called a roda in Portuguese. At Quilombola Recreation we prepare you to play with style and grace, in the manner developed by Mestre Suassuna, the founder of the acclaimed Cordão de Ouro capoeira group in Brazil.

What does Quilombola mean?

A quilombola is a resident of a quilombo in Brazil. They are the descendents of Afro-Brazilian slaves who escaped from slave plantations that existed in Brazil until abolition in 1888.

What is a Quilombo?

A quilombo is a Brazilian hinterland settlement founded by people of African origin including the Quilombolas, or, as we call them in Jamaica, Maroons.

What is Cordão de Ouro?

Cordão de Ouro, which is commonly abbreviated "CDO", was the name given to a legendary fighter in Brazil. It is also the name of Munair's capoeira group, the capoeira group started by Mestre Suassuna

What does Instrutor mean?

It is a Portuguese word meaning instructor. The title is bestowed upon a student that has attained a level of proficiency in the practice of Capoeira and the teaching of Capoeira to beginners. Zumbi was awarded the title of instrutor in 2010 from Mestre Chicote.

What does Zumbi mean?

When Munair started learning capoeira, a capoeira master called Mestre Marcelo Caveirinha, famous for making the character Eddy Gordo in the video game Tekken 3, gave him the capeoira nickname "Zumbi" after the famous Brazilian freedom fighter. The original Zumbi (1655 - November 20, 1695), also known as Zumbi dos Palmares, was the last of the leaders of the Quilombo dos Palmares, in the present-day state of Alagoas, Brazil.
